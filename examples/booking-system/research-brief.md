# Research Brief — Meeting Room Booking

## Problem Statement
- **Who has the problem:** Small offices (10-40 people, 1-3 meeting rooms) with no dedicated admin/facilities staff
- **What they do today instead:** A shared physical whiteboard, a group chat message ("anyone using room B at 2pm?"), or a shared spreadsheet nobody updates consistently
- **Cost of not solving it:** Double-booked rooms, meetings starting late while people hunt for an open room, minor daily friction that adds up — `[assumed]` no hard number without a specific office's data
- **What success looks like:** Anyone can see room availability and book a slot in under 15 seconds, from their phone or laptop

## Job to Be Done
- **Functional job:** Find an open room for a specific time window and claim it, without asking anyone
- **Emotional job:** Not want to feel like they're "bothering" a colleague or admin to check availability
- **Social job:** Be seen as someone who plans ahead, not someone who barges into occupied rooms

## Market Sanity Check
- **Top-down estimate:** `[assumed]` Small-office booking/scheduling tools are a well-established SaaS category (Calendly-adjacent, room-booking add-ons inside Google Workspace/Microsoft 365) — this MVP does not compete on market size, it demonstrates the pipeline on a problem every classroom already recognizes
- **Confidence:** Low — this is a teaching example, not a real go/no-go market decision. Treat every number above as illustrative, not a business case.
